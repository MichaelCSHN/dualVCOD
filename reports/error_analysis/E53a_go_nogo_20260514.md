## Diagnosis

E-53a is worse than E-51 on EVERY metric except white_tailed_ptarmigan.
The s4 dense supervision pathway degraded rather than helped.

Likely root causes:
1. **FPN gradient disruption**: lat1/smooth1 create an additional gradient path
   from p1→p2 that interfered with the well-tuned FPN hierarchy. The c1 features
   (stride-4, 24ch) fed upward through interpolation may have injected noisy gradients.
2. **Loss dominance**: Combined dense weight 0.75 (s4=0.25 + s8=0.5) vs E-51's 0.5.
   Even at half-weight, the s4 BCE contributed ~49% of dense loss, diverting gradient
   budget from bbox regression.
3. **Severe pygmy degradation**: The model basically stopped detecting pygmy_seahorse_0
   (0.008 IoU). This suggests the s4 head may have learned a shortcut that suppressed
   the high-frequency spatial features pygmy_seahorse needs.

## Decision

**E-53 direction CLOSED.** Do NOT proceed to E-53b 30ep.
Multi-scale dense supervision at stride-4 is not viable with this FPN architecture.
