## 4. Analysis

### 4.1 Dense Supervision Does NOT Raise the 30ep Ceiling

| Comparison | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| E-40 vs E-31 (30ep) | 0.3087 < 0.3111 → **−0.0024** |
| E-40 vs E-39 (8ep) | 0.3087 < 0.3096 → **−0.0009** |

The 30ep ceiling with dense_fg is **slightly below** the 30ep ceiling without it, and **marginally below** the 8ep dense_fg result. E-40 took 18 epochs to reach 0.3087 — E-39 reached 0.3096 in 8 epochs.

### 4.2 The LR Schedule Is the Dominant Factor

E-39's fast decay (T_max=8, LR→0 at epoch 8) "locks in" the dense_fg benefit early, producing 0.3096 at epoch 8. E-40's slow decay (T_max=30) keeps the model in high-LR regime through epoch 8, where val mIoU oscillates violently (0.22–0.26). The model only stabilizes above 0.29 after epoch 10 when LR drops below 0.00075.

**Mechanism hypothesis**: With fast LR decay, the dense_fg auxiliary loss provides a strong early regularization signal that gets "baked in" before overfitting begins. With slow decay, the model spends more epochs at high LR where the dense branch and bbox branch compete for gradient direction, causing instability. By the time LR drops enough for stabilization, the overfitting trajectory is already set.

### 4.3 Train-Val Gap Worsens with Dense Supervision at 30ep

| Trial | Train mIoU (final) | Val mIoU (best) | Gap |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|-----|
| E-31 (30ep direct_bbox) | ~0.80 (est) | 0.3111 | ~0.49 |
| E-39 (8ep dense_fg) | 0.7660 | 0.3096 | 0.456 |
| E-40 (30ep dense_fg) | 0.8536 | 0.3087 | **0.545** |

E-40 has the widest train-val gap. The dense supervision improved training fit (0.8536 is the highest train mIoU seen) but did NOT translate to validation — the gap widened by ~0.09 vs E-39.

### 4.4 R@0.5 Remains Higher Than E-31-Era Baselines

E-40 R@0.5: 0.2259 (best), 0.2121 (final) — still above E-28/E-38 levels (~0.196-0.198). The box quality improvement from dense supervision is real, even if mIoU ceiling isn't raised.

## 7. Recommendation

### 7.1 dense_fg_aux Status: Useful for fast iteration, not for mainline adoption

dense_fg_aux at 8ep is the most sample-efficient configuration tested (3.75× faster to reach ~0.31 than 30ep direct_bbox). This makes it valuable for **rapid prototyping** of other hypotheses (backbone, augmentation, loss functions). However, it should NOT replace direct_bbox as the 30ep mainline, since it does not raise the asymptotic ceiling.

### 7.2 Next Recommended Experiment: Label/Data Quality Audit

The stubborn ~0.31 ceiling across ALL approaches (DIoU, DIoU+dense_fg, DIoU+coswrp, DIoU+wd, DIoU+jitter) at 30ep strongly suggests a **data quality bottleneck** rather than an architectural one. All training improvements increase train mIoU (0.75→0.85) but leave val mIoU stuck at 0.29-0.31.

**E-41 proposal: Validation error analysis**
- Identify worst-performing video sequences in MoCA val set
- Check for: annotation noise (loose/tight bboxes), motion blur, extreme camouflage, occlusion
- Compute per-video mIoU distribution — if 10-20% of videos account for 80% of errors, targeted data cleanup or augmentation may be more impactful than architectural changes
- Check if MoCA val set contains videos from different distribution than train (different camera, resolution, object size)
- Inspect whether "empty_pred_rate=0.0" is because model always predicts something, masking a recall precision trade-off

This is a diagnostic step, not a new training experiment. It costs ~1 hour of manual inspection + script-based metrics, and could redirect the entire optimization strategy toward data quality instead of model architecture.
